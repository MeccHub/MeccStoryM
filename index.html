▸ Mastercrafting Ancient (Inherit vs Necro) (click to expand)
Q: What does Mastercraft do?
A: Mastercraft upgrades a Mythic into one of two endgame paths: Inherit (Ancient → Chaos → AbsoLab) or Necro (Ancient Necro → AbsoLab directly). This is the main progression fork in MapleStory M.
PathRole
InheritFast progression, lower RNG ceiling
NecroSlow progression, higher RNG ceiling
UpgradeSuccess Rate
Mythic → Inherit (Ancient)30% (fixed)
Inherit → Chaos~30% base → 40% refined
Mythic → Necro4% base → 8% refined
PathExpected attempts
Inherit~3–5 tries
Necro~13–20+ tries (RNG heavy)
Q: Should I refine before Mastercraft?
A: It is always worth refining before a Necro attempt for added success rate.
PathRefinement effect
Inherit❌ No effect (always 30%)
Chaos✅ ~+10% success
Necro✅ Small boost (very important)
Why refinement matters for Necro: base success is extremely low (4%), so even small increases bring large efficiency gains.
When refinement is NOT worth it: temporary gear, Inherit-only strategy, or sell/flip gear (Chaos route).
Current meta: Necro is best for endgame; Inherit is mainly a bridge/economic tool.
For new SOLO players: refine + inherit for fast access, while building Necro in parallel as your long-term project. Don’t over-invest in inherited gear.
For new SOCIAL players: if you have carries, aim for a Necro weapon ASAP to reach 2% contribution for boss loot; inherit other gear for faster survivability while progressing toward AbsoLab.
Main progression paths:
• Path A — Long-term optimal: Mythic → Necro
• Path B — Safe progression: Mythic → Inherit → Chaos
• Path C — Economic: Inherit → Chaos → sell → buy Necro
Q: Can I skip Inherit?
A: Yes. Inherit is not mandatory; it mainly accelerates progression.
Common mistakes:
1) Over-investing in Inherit (hard transition to Necro later).
2) Attempting Necro without scroll support (material-inefficient when attempts are limited).
When should I attempt Necro?
Prioritize attempts when you have Lucky Mastercraft Scrolls (often from Path of Proof/events), because higher boosted rates greatly reduce cost/time.
Key mechanics:
• Exaltation: earlier “mythic 40 first” advantage is reduced due to exalt stone crafting updates.
• MDC (Max Damage Cap): Necro scales much better at high MDC in late game.
Simple decision rule:
• Want fast power → Inherit
• Want better endgame → Necro
• Want balance → Hybrid (Inherit + Necro side project)
Ultra-concise summary: Inherit = 30% success, no refinement impact, midgame tool. Necro = low base chance but better endgame scaling. Plan Necro early and avoid overcommitting to Inherit.

▸ B ATK vs CD - Emblems and Stats (click to expand)
1) What do the two emblems actually do?
TypeCore effectScalingCoverage
Crit Emblems (Ruthless — Crit DMG%)Increase damage on crit hitsScales with Crit RateAll content (bossing + mobbing)
Boss ATK Emblems (Domination — Boss ATK%)Increase all damage vs bosses (crit + non-crit)Scales with Skill Damage %Boss-only optimization
2) Formula view
Crit scaling:
Avg ≈ Base × [1 + C × (0.2 + CD)]
Boss ATK scaling:
Boss Multiplier = (1 + ATK% + SkillDMG × BossATK%)
3) Which is better overall? Default = Crit DMG emblems. They apply to all content, scale very well at high Crit Rate, and behave closer to a global multiplier in optimized builds. Boss ATK is more situational.
4) Exact breakeven formula
Let: C = CritRate, CD = CritDMG, A = ATK%, S = SkillDMG/100, B = BossATK, ΔCD = Crit DMG gain, ΔB = Boss ATK gain.
Crit gain: (C × ΔCD) / (1 + C × (0.2 + CD))
Boss gain: (S × ΔB) / (1 + A + S × B)

Crit > Boss if:
(C × ΔCD) / (1 + C × (0.2 + CD)) > (S × ΔB) / (1 + A + S × B)
5) Example (100% Crit Rate, 250% Skill Damage)
Using real emblem values: Ruthless Lv5 = +20% CD, Domination Lv5 = +10% Boss ATK.
Crit: 0.20 / 3.2 = +6.25%
Boss ATK: 0.25 / 4.5 ≈ +5.56%
→ In this common setup, Crit wins.
6) Why earlier math sometimes shows Boss ATK winning
If you assume equal increases (ex: +20% vs +20%), Boss ATK can win in some models due to high Skill% and lower saturation in that bucket. But that assumption is unrealistic because emblem caps are not equal.
7) Real emblem scalingMax value
Crit DMG (Ruthless)+20%
Boss ATK (Domination)+10%
8) Why Crit emblems are usually more worth it
A) Higher stat per emblem slot (+20 vs +10)
B) Works everywhere (bossing, farming, dailies, multi-target)
C) Scales with Crit Rate (near-global impact at high crit consistency)
D) Boss ATK sits in an additive bucket and can saturate faster
E) Better long-term scaling as progression rises
9) When Boss ATK can be better
Low Crit Rate, very high Skill %, Crit DMG already saturated, and pure boss-only optimization. This is a narrower min-max scenario, not general-use default.
10) Does hit count matter? Yes. Higher hit count improves crit consistency and tends to increase effective Crit value. Low hit count can make Boss ATK relatively stronger.
11) Practical decision guideBest choice
General gameplayCrit
High Crit RateCrit
Early / low Crit RateBoss ATK viable
Boss-only min-maxDepends on your current buckets
Very high Skill %Boss ATK gains value
12) Final conclusion
• Crit DMG emblems = default, scalable, universal
• Boss ATK emblems = situational optimization

Even when Boss ATK can win in specific theoretical setups, Crit DMG is typically more efficient and flexible in real builds.
